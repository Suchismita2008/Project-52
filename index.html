Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleon

  • Their feet work like Salad Tongs.
  • Chameleons vary wildly in terms of size.
  • Skin crystals enable them to change colour at will.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons have been present with humans since ages. The pigeons have been around humans for thousands of year.
  • Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.
  • Pigeons are considered to be one of the most intelligent birds on the planet and able to undertake tasks previously thought to be the sole preserve of humans and primates.

Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Panda

  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • Cubs are well protected in their first month.

Flying Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.
